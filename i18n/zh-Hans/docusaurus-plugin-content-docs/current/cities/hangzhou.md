**Hangzhou**

Hangzhou, the capital of Zhejiang Province, is one of China's most renowned cultural and educational centers, attracting students and tourists alike with its perfect blend of natural beauty and academic excellence.

**Overview**
Hangzhou is a city where ancient civilization harmoniously coexists with modern innovation. As the economic and cultural hub of China's southeastern Yangtze River Delta, it offers students exceptional opportunities for academic development while immersed in one of China's most picturesque settings, famously described as "Paradise on Earth."

**Top Universities**
- **Zhejiang University** - One of China's most prestigious C9 League universities
- **Hangzhou Dianzi University** - Renowned for information technology and electronics
- **Zhejiang University of Technology** - Leading institution in engineering and technology
- **China Academy of Art** - Premier fine arts academy in China
- **Zhejiang Chinese Medical University** - Specialized in traditional Chinese medicine

**Living in Hangzhou**
**Cost of Living**
- Accommodation: ¥2,500-6,000 RMB/month depending on location
- Food: ¥1,200-2,000 RMB/month  
- Transportation: ¥150-350 RMB/month

**Climate**
- Subtropical monsoon climate with four distinct seasons
- Spring offers mild temperatures and blooming flowers
- Autumn provides the most pleasant weather with clear skies

**Transportation**
- Comprehensive subway system covering main urban areas
- Extensive bus network throughout the city
- Xiaoshan International Airport connecting to major destinations
- High-speed rail hub with Asia's largest railway station

**Cultural Attractions**
- West Lake Cultural Landscape (UNESCO World Heritage)
- Grand Canal (UNESCO World Heritage)
- Lingyin Temple (Ancient Buddhist sanctuary)
- Xixi National Wetland Park
- Hefang Street (Traditional commercial street)
- Liangzhu Ancient City (UNESCO World Heritage)
