# Shanghai

Shanghai is China's largest city and a global financial center, offering international students a unique blend of Eastern and Western cultures.

## Overview

Shanghai is a modern metropolis that has grown from a small fishing village to one of the world's most important cities. It's known for its stunning skyline, vibrant nightlife, and thriving business environment.

## Top Universities

- **Fudan University** - One of China's most selective and prestigious universities
- **Shanghai Jiao Tong University** - Strong in engineering and medicine
- **Tongji University** - Renowned for architecture and civil engineering
- **East China Normal University** - Leading teacher education institution

## Living in Shanghai

### Cost of Living
- Accommodation: ¥4,000-10,000 RMB/month depending on location
- Food: ¥2,000-3,000 RMB/month
- Transportation: ¥300-500 RMB/month

### Climate
- Humid subtropical climate with four distinct seasons
- Hot, humid summers and mild, damp winters
- Spring and autumn are comfortable

### Transportation
- World-class metro system
- Extensive bus network
- Maglev train connecting to Pudong International Airport

## Cultural Attractions

- The Bund - Historic waterfront area
- Yu Garden - Traditional Chinese garden
- Shanghai Tower - Tallest building in China
- French Concession - Historic European-style neighborhood
- Nanjing Road - Premier shopping street

## Why Study in Shanghai?

1. **Global Business Hub** - Home to numerous multinational corporations
2. **Modern Lifestyle** - Experience the most cosmopolitan city in China
3. **Career Opportunities** - Excellent job prospects for graduates
4. **Cultural Diversity** - Large international community
5. **Innovation Center** - At the forefront of China's economic development
