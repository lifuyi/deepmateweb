## Harbin Institute of Technology - International Student Handbook

### University Overview
Harbin Institute of Technology was founded in 1920 and is located in Harbin. It is a national key university directly under the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology of the People's Republic of China, with strong capabilities in engineering, technology, architecture and other fields.

### International Student Application Requirements
- **Undergraduate Application**: High school diploma, HSK Level 4 (180+ points) or equivalent Chinese proficiency
- **Graduate Application**: Bachelor's degree with relevant academic background, HSK Level 5 (180+ points) or equivalent
- **English-taught Programs**: TOEFL (80+) or IELTS (6.0+) scores required
- **Application Timeline**: Undergraduate programs typically open applications September-November

### Recommended Programs
1. **Aerospace Engineering**: Top-tier in China
2. **Mechanical Engineering**: Traditional strength
3. **Materials Science and Engineering**: Strong faculty and resources
4. **Computer Science and Technology**: Rapidly developing
5. **Civil Engineering**: Distinctive specialty

### Career Prospects
HIT graduates have an employment rate of approximately 94%, with main career paths including:
- High-tech enterprises
- Aerospace and aviation enterprises
- Government agencies
- Entrepreneurial ventures
- Further studies domestically and internationally

### Study Costs and Fees
- **Tuition**: Engineering programs approximately RMB 10,000-15,000 per year
- **Accommodation**: Student dormitory approximately RMB 1,200-2,000 per year
- **Living Expenses**: Harbin approximately RMB 1,000-1,800 per month

### Scholarship Opportunities
- China Scholarship Council (CSC) Scholarships
- Heilongjiang Province International Student Scholarships
- HIT Special Scholarships
- Corporate-sponsored Scholarships

### Living Guide - Harbin
- Harbin is the provincial capital of Heilongjiang, known as the "Ice and Snow City"
- HIT campus offers beautiful surroundings
- Harbin winters are cold and require warm clothing
- Cost of living is relatively lower; abundant winter sports and ice tourism opportunities
