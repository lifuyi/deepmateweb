# Shenzhen

Shenzhen is China's technology and innovation capital, often called the "Silicon Valley of China."

## Overview

Located in southern China, Shenzhen has transformed from a small fishing village to a global technology powerhouse in just a few decades. It's now home to some of the world's largest tech companies and startups.

## Top Universities

- **Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech)** - Young but rapidly rising in rankings
- **Shenzhen University** - Comprehensive university with strong industry connections
- **Harbin Institute of Technology (Shenzhen)** - Top engineering school
- **Peking University Shenzhen Graduate School** - Extension of China's top university

## Living in Shenzhen

### Cost of Living
- Accommodation: ¥3,500-9,000 RMB/month depending on location
- Food: ¥2,000-3,000 RMB/month
- Transportation: ¥200-400 RMB/month

### Climate
- Subtropical monsoon climate
- Warm and humid year-round
- Mild winters, hot summers

### Transportation
- Modern metro system
- Extensive bus network
- Close to Hong Kong with easy border crossing

## Cultural Attractions

- Window of the World - Theme park featuring world landmarks
- Shenzhen Museum - Learn about the city's rapid development
- Dafen Oil Painting Village - Art community
- OCT-LOFT Creative Culture Park - Art galleries and cafes
- Dameisha and Xiaomeisha beaches - Coastal recreation

## Why Study in Shenzhen?

1. **Tech Hub** - Center of China's technology industry
2. **Innovation Ecosystem** - Numerous startups and tech giants
3. **Proximity to Hong Kong** - Easy access to international business
4. **Youthful City** - Young population with vibrant energy
5. **Career Opportunities** - Excellent prospects in technology sector
